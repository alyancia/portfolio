Course: Principles of Engineering, 2016-2017

Name of Project: Mechanical Winch System

Duration: 1 week

Objective: To design and create a lifting mechanism to move heavy objects up 3 floors.

Description:

To lift heavy objects up three floors, a team of four was tasked to build a mechanism out of VEX or FT parts and to program a NetLogo stimulation. This project was very fast paced and also challenging. Occasionally, our stimulation would stop working because of a typing error and the system would be unable to read it. We also had to constantly adjust our original design because there was a lack of parts.

In this project, I was mainly the documenter and the programmer. As the documenter, I did a majority of the summaries and annotations for the pictures and did most of the calculations. As the programmer, I programmed a large portion of the stimulation. Though I did participate in the design process of the vehicle, I did not physically participate in the building process. Course: Principles of Engineering, 2016-2017

Name of Project: Solar Hydrogen Vehicle

Duration: 3 weeks

Objective: To design, create, and test a vehicle that runs on solar or hydrogen fuel cells.

Description:

Because of the decreasing amount of non renewable fuel cells, engineers have been coming up with ways for vehicles to fun on alternative energy. In this project, a team of four were to design and test a vehicle built using VEX or FT materials that ran on solar or hydrogen fuel cells. Though the solar cells were easy and convenient to use, we ran into many challenges with the hydrogen cell. There were occasions where a fully charged hydrogen cell would not move the vehicle, even though there were no issues with the vehicle's mechanics. However, we realized that we needed to change our gear ratio for the sprocket system and after the problem was resolved, the hydrogen power vehicle was running.

In this project, I was mainly the documenter where I worked on calculations, annotations, and summation of data. I did participate in the design process of the vehicle; however, did not physically build too much of it. For further information on the project, the document link is available. Please note that the highlighted portions are the sections I took the most leadership role in or had the most team work in.

Course: Principles of Engineering, 2016-2017

Name of Project: West Point Bridge Design

Duration: 1 week

Objective: To design and test a stable, 2 lane, and low cost bridge

Description:

Bridges are needed to cross valleys, rivers, and many different landscapes. In this project, a team of one to three was tasked to design and test a stable, 2 lane, and low cost bridge using a stimulation program called West Point Bridge Design. There were not many challenges in this project as most of it was testing different bridge designs. We had to switch many of the materials we originally wanted to use of the bridge design to reduce the cost. It was difficult to find a perfect balance of cost and stability but after experimenting with the system, we managed to find a stable yet low cost bridge.

In this project, I assisted in the designing and testing of bridges and documented a large portion of the project. I designed the final design solution and typed up the design modifications along with the final design summary. For further information on the project, the document link is available. Course: Principles of Engineering, 2016-2017

Name of Project: Machine Control

Duration: 2 weeks

Objective: To design and build a delivery vehicle that will move back and forth based on potentiometer input.

Description:

In this project, a team of four was tasked to solve one of eight design problems using only VEX or FT materials and RobotC. The problem that our team decided to address was to make a delivery vehicle that moves back and forth based on a potentiometer input. During this project, we faced many challenges. We had to find enough wire to connect the motors and the buttons on the vehicle to the separate remote that we had created and later had to connect all of them to their correct ports. Furthermore, we had issues with our program that we needed to constantly address to make the vehicle move. We often had to think of the stability and strength of the vehicle because it ultimately had to carry something on its back; therefore, many of our designs had to be altered to accommodate this.

In this project, I was mainly the documenter and the tester. I typed up the designs, the design modifications, final solution summary and the annotations for most of the pictures. I also tested the prototype many times to make sure that the product works. Though I participated less in the building process, I contributed to the design process.
