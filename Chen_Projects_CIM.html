Name of Project: Inch Cubed Project

Duration: 2-3 weeks

Objective: To design something of our choice and 3D print it onto one cubic inch of material or less.

Description:

This project was an individual project and was designed to help us explore more about the 3D printing process and learn its limitations. This project required a lot of creativity and fast learning to adjust to a new machine.

In this project, I brainstormed several ideas I had in my engineering notebook and sketched each of them out. I wanted something creative and detailed, so I chose the biohazard symbol design I drew. I faced challenges through out this project because it was my first time using inventor so I had to constantly ask those around me for help. However, I quickly learned the process and adjusted to it. My original design was a one sided design but I thought it would more intricate and interesting if I did the symbol on both sides of the circle plate. Once I printed the design and put it in the tank to allow the support material to disolve, the part was done. I did the entire documentation myself, including the original brainstorming design, the AutoDesk Inventor design, and then the picture of the final product. Course: Computer Integrated Manufacturing, 2018-2019

Name of Project: Laser Cut Game Design

Duration: 3 week

Objective: To design and laser cut game

Description:

In this project, we were tasked to work in teams of two or three to design and build a marble action game. It needed to be fabricated using the laser cutter and could be assembled using glue and VEX parts. We were each given a sheet of mat board (12” x 12” x .055”), 1 piece of ⅛” basswood (3” x 24” x .125”), 1 piece of felt (6” x 9”), 1 piece of birch tongue depressors (6” x .75” x .065), marbles (approx 9/16” dia), and rubber bands.

We decided to make a sliding picture game and found a picture of a dragon on google to make our picture out of. We cut the picture into 9 boxes to make the sliding tiles and printed it on the basswood. For the frame to put the tiles in, we used the matboard and created a square for the base and four rectangles for the perimeter, connecting it all with wood glue. Though the cutting process and the assembly process went smoothly, when we put the parts into the frame, it did not slide smoothly. Therefore, we sanded to corners of each tile so it was rounded and it slide much more smoothly. I finished the documentation on my own. Course: Computer Integrated Manufacturing, 2018-2019

Name of Project: Laser Cut Business Card

Duration: 3 weeks

Objective: To design and laser cut a business card with cuts and engraves on the material of our choice.

Description:

Using the limitations that we discovered from the first laser cut design (for ex: the amount of detail it could add), we were tasked individually to design our own business cards.

For this project, I recieved a icy blue, 3" by 2" sheet of acrylic plastic for the final product. Wanting to have the design on the business card match the color of the material, I decided to go for ice skates and snowflakes as a decorative factor. I put my name, number, and email, but later thought my business card looked empty, so I decided to add my Instagram too. I put the icons to represent each - phone, mail, and the instagram logo - and added my decorative factor next to it. I cut the snowflakes and engraved the rest. I printed a prototype on a matboard and noticed that my cuts engraved instead of cutting through. I quickly realized that I had chosen the wrong red for the places I wanted to cut; therefore, I had to go back to change the color to RGB red so that the process would complete. Once I was satisfied with the prototype, I printed the final product. For more information, the document link is avalible
