Course: Computer Science and Software Engineering, 2017-2018

Name of Project: Scratch Project: Penguin Maze

Duration: 1 week

Objective: To design and test a game that exposes players to the complications that penguins face when searching for food

Description:

Like many other animals, penguins eat fish. Because more than one species eats fish, penguins have to fight for their food. However, penguins are both predators to fish and prey to many other species. In this project, a team of two was tasked to design and test a game that exposes players to the complications that penguins face when searching for food. We experienced many different challenges trying to make the different levels. We constantly ran into problems when building the obstacles, only in the end to find out that a block was misplaced or something was not inputted correctly. In the end we developed a working and challenging game that completes our objective.

In this project, I assisted in the designing, programming, and testing of bridges and documented a large portion of the project. I contributed to the initial designing of the project, assisted in programming, typed up the majority of the documentation (Daily log and Daily Reflection, Brainstorming, Diagram/Flow-Chart, Instructions on How to Play, and my portion of the Conclusion Questions). Course: Computer Science and Software Engineering, 2017-2018

Name of Project: MIT App Inventor: Space Invaders

Duration: 2 weeks

Objective: To design and test an app that would attract users to space exploration.

Description:

In this project, a team of two was tasked to design and test an app that would attract players to play a space exploration game using MIT App Inventor. We faced many challenges in this project as the game was not working in the way that we intended. For example, we wanted to make it so that the food in the game would drop every couple cycles; however, the food would just continually drop down in every cycle. We fixed this by changing the timer and the scale in which it dropped, leaving the program able to drop the food every couple cycles.

In this project, I was mainly the programmer and the tester. I programmed a a majority of the game and came up with many of the solutions to problems that kept occuring. I also tested the game many times to make sure that the product works. Though I participated less documentation, I contributed to the initial design of the game.
